Privacy Policy for AenoxDocs

Effective date: 2026-06-05

AenoxDocs is operated by Aenox. If you have questions about this policy, contact aenoxstudio@gmail.com.

AenoxDocs is an offline-first Android PDF viewer and organizer. No account or sign-in is required. The app does not show ads.

What AenoxDocs Handles

To provide its features, AenoxDocs may handle:

  • PDFs and other document files you choose to open or import
  • document names and basic library metadata such as file size, page count, timestamps, folder assignment, and favorite status
  • local folders, sort and view preferences, appearance settings, and viewer mode settings
  • local trial and subscription entitlement state used to unlock Pro features

Local Storage

Imported PDFs are stored locally on your device in app-private storage managed by AenoxDocs.

If you open a PDF from another app or location without importing it, AenoxDocs may create a temporary local cached copy so it can be displayed reliably in the viewer.

AenoxDocs does not provide a server-side document upload or cloud sync service, and it does not upload your PDF contents to AenoxDocs servers.

Android backup is disabled for the app's data in the current Android app configuration.

How Files Leave the App

Your files leave AenoxDocs only when you choose an action such as sharing, opening a PDF with another app, or printing.

When you use those actions, the selected destination app, printer service, or Android system service may receive access to the file you selected. AenoxDocs uses Android platform sharing features, including FileProvider, for this user-initiated sharing.

Network Use

AenoxDocs is designed to work offline for core PDF viewing and organization. Network access may still be used for:

  • Google Play Billing purchase, subscription, and restore flows
  • crash and error diagnostics through Sentry
  • Google Play or Android platform services needed for app distribution and billing

AenoxDocs does not offer cloud document syncing or server-side document storage.

Google Play Billing

Pro subscriptions and related purchase flows are handled by Google Play Billing. Google Play, not AenoxDocs, processes your payment details.

AenoxDocs does not receive or store your payment card details. The app may store local entitlement and trial status on your device so subscription and trial access can work correctly offline. Google Play may retain billing and purchase records under its own policies.

Crash and Error Diagnostics

AenoxDocs uses Sentry for crash and error diagnostics so problems can be identified and fixed.

Crash reports may include technical information such as device model, Android version, app version, build type, timestamps, and stack traces. AenoxDocs does not intentionally send PDF contents to Sentry. It also does not intentionally include document filenames, file paths, or content URIs in crash reports.

Sentry retains diagnostic data under its own retention and privacy practices.

Data Sharing

AenoxDocs does not sell personal data.

AenoxDocs does not use advertising SDKs, does not build advertising profiles, and does not serve ads.

Information may be shared only:

  • with Google Play for purchases, subscriptions, and restore flows
  • with Sentry for crash and error diagnostics
  • with another app, printer service, or platform component when you choose to share, open, or print a file

Retention and Deletion

Local PDFs, folders, preferences, and other app data remain on your device until you delete them in the app, uninstall the app, clear app data, or Android removes the app's local data.

If you delete an imported PDF in AenoxDocs, the app removes its local app-managed record and associated local app-managed file according to app behavior.

If you delete a folder in AenoxDocs, the folder record is removed, but PDFs that were inside that folder are kept locally and moved back into the main library according to app behavior.

Crash diagnostic data may be retained by Sentry according to Sentry's policies. Billing and purchase records are handled by Google Play under Google's policies.

Security

AenoxDocs stores imported library files in app-private storage on your device. Network communication used for billing and crash diagnostics is intended to use standard platform and HTTPS-based service connections where applicable.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, so absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

Children

AenoxDocs is not directed to children. It is intended for general productivity use.

Your Choices

You can:

  • delete PDFs and folders in the app
  • uninstall the app or clear app data to remove local app data from the device
  • manage or cancel subscriptions through Google Play

The current app version does not provide an in-app setting to turn crash reporting on or off.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as AenoxDocs changes. The latest published version will show the current effective date.
